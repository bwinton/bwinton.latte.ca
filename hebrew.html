Hebrew Word of the Day - Issue #310

Monday July 29, 2002
20 Av, 5762

CHESHBON = bill (check after dinner in a restaurant), can also mean "mathematics"
Spelling = CHET SHIN BET NUN-SOFIT

from the root verb: HASHAV = to think, plan, intend and/or HISHEV = to calculate, reckon, computer, figure

Intermediate: MADA = science
Spelling: MEM DALET AYIN

Do you think the word "science" is used in the Hebrew Scriptures? Actually, it is used once in Daniel 1:4. "skillful in all wisdom, and cunnig in knowledge, and understanding science, ... " From the verb: YADAH (YOD DALET AYIN) meaning "to know". Note also "MADUA" means "Why?"

Hebrew Word of the Day - Issue #304

Thursday May 2, 2002
20 Iyar, 5762

Beginner: GAMAL = camel
Spelling: GIMEL MEM LAMED

Can you see how the English word "camel" is like the Hebrew word "GAMAL"? The initial letter "C" and "G" are very similar.
GAMAL is also related to the Hebrew letter GIMEL, which might be though to be shaped like a camel.

Intermediate: D'VORAH = bee
Spelling: DALET VET VAV RESH HEI

The English name "Deborah" comes from this word.

Hebrew Word of the Day - Issue #298 Tues March 26, 2002
13 Nisan, 5762

CHAG SAMEACH = Happy Holiday (Pesach = Passover)

The word PESACH (spelled PEH SAMECH CHET) comes from the verb PASACH meaning: pass-over, spring-over.

Beginner: SEH = lamb
Spelling: SIN HEI

There are other words for sheep and lamb, but this is the one used in the Passover passage: Exodus 12:4. It has been said that a language has many words for what is important to it. For example, Arabic has over 20 words for camel, and Eskimoes have over 70 words for snow.

Intermediate: EZ = goat
Spelling: AYIN ZAYIN
