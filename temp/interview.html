An Interview With Bwinton.

Interviewed by Columbine
December 14, 2003

Answer using complete sentences. Use extra paper if necessary.

  1. OK, it strikes me that the first and foremost significant factor in your life, at least at the moment, is that you have this amazing small human creature in your environment. Tell me about fatherhood. Tell me about the ways it's changed your life, for good or bad, including the surprises nobody warned you about. In fact, especially those. Tell me about the way it's changed your relationship with the other adults in your life as well - not just your wife but friends, other couples, family, strangers on the street, anything that comes to mind ....

    There haven't been any truly major changes in my life since Del arrived, which I attribute to my being a fairly boring person. I seem to have gotten all the drinking, smoking, and drunken carousing out of my system in University, and since then I've been a big, lazy, homebody. In that respect, the major change Del has introduced is that I now have a good excuse to leave a party early or kick people out of my house. Another major life change, as you might have noticed from my blinking red name on Lisa's interviewee's page is that I have much less free time now than I used to, so the simplest of tasks, like answering five interview questions, for instance, takes me far, far longer than I think it will. To be fair to Del I didn't really do that much with my free time before she came, but now that she's here I seem to be looking back fondly on the copious amounts of spare time I used to have. I have noticed that I'm always thnking about Del when I'm with her, and what I can do to make her life better or easier, and that's a bit of a change, but I was brought up in a household where anticipating other people's needs was the norm, so it's not as big a change as it might be. (My father would get very upset when we couldn't read his mind, and bring him what he needed before he asked for it. He was a little insane back then, but he's gotten a lot better these days, and can laugh about it when we throw it back in his face.)

    Other than that, I think everyone warned us about any possible thing that could go wrong, and a bunch of things that couldn't, but I didn't really pay much attention to them for a few reasons. Because there was so much advice and suggestions of things to do, and so many warnings of things that we should not do, I couldn't possibly hope to remember them all, and since one of my main strengths is that I think well on my feet, I figured that even if when I didn't know what was wrong, I would be able to handle it at least as well as if I had memorized all of the advice.

    Further, I've seen a lot of advice given, and most of it has been bad (story about Morgan & Mom & toothpaste on a wasp bite) so I'm generally inclined to nod politely and go doing what I was going to do. And finally, pretty much everyone who gave us recommendations brought their own baggage to the table, very little of which seems to apply to Delphine, since she is a far better baby than we could have hoped for, so most of the things we would have prepared for didn't happen. The one thing that I was warned about, which I didn't really understand at the time, is how completely I love her. Rob said it better than I can, but I would and will do anything in my power to protect her and make her life as fun as it can be.
