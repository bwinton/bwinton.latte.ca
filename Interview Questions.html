1. OK, it strikes me that the first and foremost significant factor in your life, at least at the moment, is that you have this amazing small human creature in your environment. Tell me about fatherhood. Tell me about the ways it's changed your life, for good or bad, including the surprises nobody warned you about. In fact, especially those. Tell me about the way it's changed your relationship with the other adults in your life as well - not just your wife but friends, other couples, family, strangers on the street, anything that comes to mind ....

A. That wasn't a question. No real changes, boring person before, Del's just a good excuse to leave early/kick people out. Less free time, not that I did much with it. Always looking out for someone else, but brought up in a household where that was the norm, just thinking about it differently. I think everyone warned us about all the stuff that would happen, and a lot more besides, but I didn't pay much attention, for a few reasons. Because there was so much advice and warnings I couldn't possibly hope to remember them all, and since I think well on my feet, I figured that even if when I didn't know what was wrong, I would be able to handle it, I didn't even bother trying to remember any of it. Further, I've seen a lot of advice given, and most of it has been bad (story about Morgan & Mom & toothpaste on a wasp bite) so I'm generally inclined to nod politely and go doing what I was going to do. And finally, pretty much everyone who gave us recommendations brought their own baggage to the table, very little of which seems to apply to Delphine, since she is a far better baby than we could have hoped for, so most of the things we would have prepared for didn't happen. The one thing that I was warned about, which I didn't really understand at the time, is how completely I love her. Rob said it better than I can, but I would and will do anything in my power to protect her and make her life as fun as it can be.
